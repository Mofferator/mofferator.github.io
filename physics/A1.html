A1: Mechanics

Vectors and Scalars

All physical properties are either scalar or vector.

for example:

Vectors Scalars
Velocity Speed
Force Mass
Displacement Distance

Vectors can have both magnitude and direction where scalars only have magnitude, this means that scalars cannot have negative values (e.g. you can't have a negative mass)

Resolving Vectors

Vectors can be resolved into the coordinate axes using trigonometry, this mean we can gain independent values of the vector for each of the cartesian directions. We do this by treating the vector itself as a hypotenuse of a right angled triangle, with the opposite and adjacent directions being in the directions of the coordinate axes (or paralled/perpendicular to a different plane if it's more convenient.

Resolving vectors is very useful for calculations where we want to isolate the different direction of a vector, e.g. finding the vertical component of a velocity for projectile calculations.
